Deep Oar Fish

Energy: 1 Value: 3 Cook Time: 0

Raw: Heavy.
If cooked instead, +1 value.

Common / Seafood
[Their flavour comes out through cooking.]

[Deep Oarfish is an exploration of Raw as a binary decision choice for players rather than the 'use-it-or-lose-it' maxim. By providing a value reward after cooking, it gives them more breathing room so they don't feel as bad if they lose the Raw bonus, a more tactical aspect (planning ahead of turns by using both rewards), and more opportunities for decision-making. This, I feel, is an example of how deep Raw as a mechanic can be.]

Common / Vegetable
[A river spirit's favorite meal.]

[Ben Brode, previous lead design for Blizzard's Hearthstone, often said that card complexities and power levels existed on a spectrum. Legendary cards *feel* legendary because there are cards that suck that you can compare them to. It could just be a flimsy excuse to make pack fillers. Or an excuse for bad cards. But I think there's an element of truth to it. Intentionally-designed boring or bad cards can sometimes elevate the experience. Like an almost pause of a musical bar in an orchestra that's going full steam ahead. Won't lie to you though, was pretty uninspired when making the cucumber.]
